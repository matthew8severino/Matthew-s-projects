Apple Pie

With every cinnamon-sweet bite, this golden pie melts hearts and stirs feelings. One slice, and your crush might just fall—harder than the apples in it.

Ingredients

  • 6 medium apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp), peeled and sliced
  • ½ cup white sugar
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon butter (to dot on top of filling)

Instructions

  1. Make the crust:
    Mix flour and salt. Cut in butter until crumbly. Add ice water slowly until dough comes together. Divide in two, wrap in plastic, and chill for 1 hour.
  2. Make the filling:
    Combine sliced apples, sugars, cinnamon, nutmeg, flour, and lemon juice. Mix well.
  3. Assemble the pie:
    Roll out one dough half and line a 9-inch pie pan. Add apple filling and dot with butter. Roll out the second dough half and cover the pie (or make a lattice).
  4. Add finishing touches:
    Brush the crust with beaten egg and sprinkle with sugar.
  5. Bake:
    Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F). Bake for 15 minutes, then reduce heat to 175°C (350°F) and bake for another 35–40 minutes, until golden and bubbly.
  6. Cool and serve:
    Let cool for at least 1 hour before slicing.
